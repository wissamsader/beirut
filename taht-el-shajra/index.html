yes, there's a real tree
The name is literal

A courtyard, a canopy,
and time slowing down

Taht El Shajra — “under the tree” — is exactly that: a cozy Hamra corner where sidewalk tables gather beneath real branches, the Arabic coffee is rich and correct, and the kitchen quietly invents dishes you won't find anywhere else. People walk in for the shade and fall in love with the place instantly.

By day the sidewalk tables catch the Hamra light; by night the branches glow green over the courtyard, and nobody remembers what they were rushing to do.

House rule: the coffee is Arabic, the halloum comes with figs and walnuts, and the tree stays.
