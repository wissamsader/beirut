The time capsule

Beirut, 1960-something, preserved with love

Kahwet 60 turned a Pasteur-street rooftop into a time machine: pink walls and green shutters, floral sofas, copper rakwehs on crochet doilies, and a breakfast spread that arrives like a color TV test card. The decade never ended here — it just got a better view of the city.

Floral sofas, wall clocks that all agree to disagree, copper rakwehs and Turkish delight on crochet doilies — every corner styled like your grandmother's best salon.

Now playing: the classicsFairuz in the morning, tarab after dark — as the law requires.
Sky-high vibes

A balcony over the whole city

The rooftop rail looks straight across Beirut's rooftops — orange juice at sunrise, shisha smoke curling at sunset, and that pink wall glowing behind you in every photo.
