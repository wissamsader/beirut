The name

A family called The Golden, for a reason

In Barbour, the Zahabi name — “the golden” — is a promise kept daily: trays of knafeh leaving the oven, baklava glistening in rows, maamoul b'ashta and qarabij weighed out with the extraordinary generosity the neighborhood swears by. Everything perfect, everything golden, 7:30 in the morning till 10 at night.

Mad ashta, qarabij, maamoul b'ashta, knafeh at dawn — the counter serves Noueiri and Barbour the way a goldsmith serves a bride: precisely, generously, and never in a hurry.
